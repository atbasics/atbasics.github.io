Who uses it?

Anyone can use assistive technology. Whether an individual has a disability, chronic illness, is elderly or not, is blind, has low vision, is hard of hearing, or does not relate to any of these, assistive technology is part of our everyday lives. We may not realize it, but we use assistive technology in our everyday lives! The Alexa sitting on your table at home, the glasses you wear to see, a pencil grip holder, a white cane, and a wheelchair. These are just a few examples of assistive technology that individuals use but may not realize.
