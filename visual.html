Visual

Visual impairments refer to conditions that affect a person’s ability to see, ranging from mild vision loss to total blindness. These may be present at birth or acquired over time due to aging, injury, or disease. According to the World Health Organization (WHO), at least 2.2 billion people have near or distance vision impairment, and half of these cases could have been prevented or are yet to be addressed.

Blind/Low Vision

Visual impairment is a term that describes any degree of vision loss that cannot be fully corrected with glasses, contact lenses, medication, or surgery. It includes moderate and severe visual impairment, as well as total blindness.​

Types of Visual Impairment

Uncorrected vision: Vision loss that has not been addressed with corrective lenses or treatment.
Moderate visual impairment: Reduced vision that makes daily tasks challenging, even with corrective lenses.
Severe visual impairment: Significantly limited vision that impacts most activities, even with correction.
Blindness: Very limited or no vision; may include light perception only or no visual perception at all.

Assistive Technology for Low Vision and Blindness

Low Vision:
 - Screen magnifiers and zoom features
 - Large-print books, labels, and materials
 - High-contrast displays and color adjustments
 - Video magnifiers (CCTVs)
 - Built-in accessibility features (Magnifier, Zoom, display settings)
 - Adjustable text size and brightness
 - Voice commands and dictation
 - Enlarged keyboards and key labels
 - Large-button phones and remote controls
 - High-contrast clocks, watches, and timers
 - Adaptive kitchen tools with visual contrast
 - Magnifying mirrors and task lighting
 - Enlarged digital text and documents
 - Visual organizers and accessible learning platforms
 - Screen magnification software
Blindness:
 - Screen readers that read text out loud
 - Text-to-speech software
 - Braille displays and Braille note-takers
 - Audiobooks and accessible digital text
 - Built-in screen readers (VoiceOver, TalkBack, NVDA, JAWS)
 - Voice commands and keyboard navigation
 - Audio feedback and shortcut keys
 - Smart home devices with voice control
 - Talking clocks, watches, and thermometers
 - Audio or tactile labeling systems
 - medication management tools
 - White canes and smart canes
 - GPS and navigation apps designed for blind users
 - Wayfinding and object-identification apps
 - Audible traffic signals
 - Accessible digital textbooks
 - Screen-reader-compatible documents
 - Note-taking tools with speech or Braille output
